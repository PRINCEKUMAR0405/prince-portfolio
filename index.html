About Me

I am Prince Kumar, a student-leader, researcher, and builder working at the convergence of Economics, Computer Science, and Sustainability. My journey reflects a consistent transition from awareness to action to leadership to system-level thinking.

With a strong foundation in analytical subjects and real-world engagement, I combine academic rigor with community impact and entrepreneurial exploration. From founding grassroots initiatives in rural India to conducting economic policy research, my work spans multiple domains but maintains a singular focus: creating scalable solutions that improve systems, empower communities, and drive meaningful change.

At Mahindra United World College of India (MUWCI), I'm pursuing the IB Diploma while leading multiple initiatives that bridge the gap between classroom learning and real-world application. My academic interests in Economics and Computer Science are not just theoretical—they're tools I use to understand complex systems and build practical solutions.

Economics provides the framework to understand systems, incentives, and human behavior at scale. Computer Science offers the tools to build solutions, analyze data, and automate processes. Together, they form a powerful combination for addressing complex global challenges.

My experience spans from designing water filters to analyzing EV policy—from grassroots implementation to system-level thinking. This interdisciplinary approach isn't just academic; it's the future of problem-solving in an increasingly interconnected world.

Career Goals
